Final Solution

During World War II, the Japanese government implemented a system called "Comfort Women" to force women into sexual slavery. They recruited women, particularly in Korea and Taiwan, through local or Japanese brokers, deceiving them with promising employment in Japan or other Japanese-occupied territories. Most comfort women were not paid and treated poorly. They were also forced to serve daily soldiers and receive an injection of salvarsan or arsphenamine to prevent Syphilis.
